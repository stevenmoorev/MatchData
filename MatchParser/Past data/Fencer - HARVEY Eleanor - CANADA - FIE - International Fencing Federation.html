Information

Further Personal Information

Occupation Athlete
Languages English
Higher education Film Studies, Psychology - Ohio State University: Columbus, OH, USA

Sport Specific Information

When and where did you begin this sport? She began fencing at age 10.
Why this sport? She was originally involved in athletics and karate. She switched to fencing on the advise of a friend, and because karate was not an Olympic sport at the time.
Club / Team Ottawa Fencing: Canada
Name of coach Paul ApSimon [national], CAN
Handedness Left

General Interest

Nicknames E (olympic.ca, 22 Aug 2016)
Hobbies Yoga, rock climbing, drawing, going to the cinema with friends, eating international cuisine, going to coffee shops. (olympic.ca, 22 Aug 2016; buckeyextra.dispatch.com, 06 Nov 2015)
Memorable sporting achievement Finishing seventh in the individual foil event at the 2016 Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro. (olympic.ca, 28 Nov 2016)
Most influential person in career Her parents. (buckeyextra.dispatch.com, 06 Nov 2015)
Sporting philosophy / motto "I'm definitely more defensive. I like to get a feel for people's styles, so that takes a while. If you're rushing at them it's harder to get a feel for what they would do in different situations." (buckeyextra.dispatch.com, 06 Nov 2015)
Awards and honours She was named the 2014 Canadian Junior Fencer of the Year. (900chml.com, 26 Feb 2015)

She has been inducted into the Toronto Fencing Club Hall of Fame in Ontario, Canada. (awwca.ca, 18 Dec 2011)
Famous relatives Her parents Ken Harvey and Lise Graydon have both taken part in Ironman triathlons. Her grandfather competed in athletics at Michigan State University in the United States of America. She is also a descendant of Dutch artist Vincent Van Gogh. (olympic.ca, 22 Aug 2016; ohiostatebuckeyes.com, 08 Apr 2015)
Ambitions To compete at the 2020 Olympic Games in Tokyo. (olympic.ca, 28 Nov 2016)
Hero / Idol Italian fencer Arianna Errigo. (eurosport.co.uk, 10 Aug 2016)
Other information NATIONAL FIRST
She became the first female Canadian fencer to win a medal in foil at a grand prix event, following her bronze medal at the 2018 event in Shanghai, People's Republic of China. (fencing.ca, 22 May 2018; SportsDeskOnline, 09 Jul 2018)

VEGAN DIET
She has been on a vegan diet since age 12. Her fencing coach at the time required her to submit blood work to prove she was getting the nutrients necessary to train and compete. "I feel like if anything [a vegan diet] helps me because you have to be super aware all the time of what you're eating, you can't just like pig out on a random thing placed in front of you. I think that's a good way to be if you're vegan or not. Better still, on this diet I always found I had more energy." (thelantern.com, 25 Oct 2017)
